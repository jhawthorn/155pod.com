Not growing up, but burning out

 

1:55 is a weekly newsletter for Patreon Gatekeepers.

As you may have heard already, one of us was jabbed last weekend and still has horse tranquilizers running through our veins instead of human blood. Do these recommendations reflect the troubled misfires of a 5G-addled brain? At least one probably does!

Let’s go.

ellis “hospital”

Sam: This song feels like something I loved but forgot from a CD-R mix I made in high school before going away to camp for the summer. Minimal but with some luuuuuush vocals, “hospital” is slow and sad in a way that still feels better suited to a warm night than anything properly depressingly cold and Canadian, which is the kind of slow sad music I do not care for. I have genre-related questions because this person is from Hamilton and we seem to have a lot of mutuals and yet? For that reason I read it as more ‘00s emo than indie rock, and that’s why I like it (okay confirmed they covered the Used so it’s emo).

Josiah: This is some nice sadsack music for sure. But Sam loves Hamilton so much he made a whole TV show about it. Sam should marry Hamilton. He should write a musical about Hamilton and call it Hamilton but make it a different one. He should change his name to Samilton.
 

Cory Todd “Highway Star (Belter Version)”

Sam: I’m super interested in the worlds that people have thrown themselves into during the pandemic as a coping mechanism. My wife got super into Vanderpump Rules and claims that she will remember this as the time she made friends with the staff of SUR. For me, it’s been a journey back to one of my earliest loves: genre fiction. I only started reading the Expanse series late last year but I’m already six books and four seasons deep - this will be the year I became friends with the crew of the Rocinante. This corny but super fun garage rock dirge plays in a very cool and pivotal moment, and I literally Shazamed it to be like “what the fuck” because I had never noticed any music in the show that actually utilized the Belter Creole that is such an engaging part of the series’ world building. I have no idea if this song is good out of context (I’m pretty sure it’s not outside of the intro and the solo) but it reminds me of my new friends in space and that rocks.

Josiah: Bro what the fuck are you talking about and why am I listening to a Deep Purple cover for cosplayers?
 

Skrillex, Starrah and Four Tet “Butterflies”

Josiah: Ever since I cancelled Apple Music for reasons, I’ve been loving the Spotify UI. Sorry artists, but Spotify rocks. I’ve been trying really hard to catch up on all the IDM I couldn’t afford when I was a teenager and could only buy CDs at HMV, but that basically just means listening to Aphex Twin over and over again, and getting bored of Autechre and Mouse on Mars. I think I’m secretly just wanting to get really into EDM instead, and maybe the distinctions between the two are dying now that neither genre is really relevant anymore. That would explain why Skrillex and Four Tet teamed up for a delightful house song that sounds like a Topman dressing room in the best possible way. And while neither artist is shining through too much, it’s hard not to hear Kieran Hebden when we get to the chimes solo towards the end.

Sam: I love that Skrillex didn’t just disappear as a highly-mocked relic of a specific moment in popular culture and instead keeps making sick music with interesting people. It wasn’t just a gimmick! I’ll always hold Sonny close to my heart because my first cease and desist notice came after my first real viral success with The Skrillex Machine. Having your name on a legal document from Skrillex’s lawyer? Kinda cool!
 

People Just Do Nothing: Big in Japan

Josiah: If you really think about it, People Just Do Nothing is a terrible show. There is basically no plot, there is absolutely no character development and the jokes are occasionally too subtle to the point where it’s hard to win people over right away. But it’s also maybe my favourite comedy of the last decade. I love the characters so much, I love their fits, I love the music (which, again, is kind of muddled because they are genuinely talented and sound so cool, but are objectively failures in the show) and I love Beats and Grindah’s relationship. I can’t stop rewatching this trailer, and I think KuruptFM (or, now, the Bang Boys) is maybe the only artist left that I badly need to see live before I die.

Sam: Friend of the pod and comp contributor Daniel Halyburton is making a new push to get all of our friends into this show in time for the movie. Can’t tell if Josiah always telling me how good it is helps or hurts.
