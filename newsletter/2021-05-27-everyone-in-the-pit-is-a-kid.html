Good riddance

 

1:55 is a weekly newsletter for Patreon Gatekeepers.

That’s a wrap on Green May, basically - we’re doing a Twitch stream to celebrate this momentous moment in all of our lives this Friday at 8pm ET. One of us (guess who) has secured an extremely stupid thing that will be very fun to watch together. And then we’re never going to talk about Green Day ever again.

Let’s go.
 

Tobe Nwigwe “Fye Fye” feat. Fat Nwigwe

Sam: I generally avoid writing, talking, or posting about hip-hop because I’m extremely a tourist, and even though I rarely know what I’m talking about with punk either I at least feel confident being an idiot and getting corrected. But holy shit, this song feels like a Fest anthem and that means it’s squarely in my wheelhouse. Sure, everyone in the pit is a kid, but aren’t we all children at heart? This is hardly an under-the-radar sneaky pick, but the first time I heard this I felt the energy of it smash me over the fucking head and knew I’d embarass myself by trying to write about it. Just listen to it and feel alive.

Josiah: Y’know, it’s been real cute to watch lil Sammy discover hip things like ambient music and gabber and, well, punk over the last few years, but this is a whole new level. He’s discovered something I already knew all about because he’s been popping off in Christian circles for a while. And he is absolutely is sick as all hell, always rapping directly into the camera and making you feel hyped up (and also, perhaps, a lil bit sanctified). Also, the mint-coloured grills in this video are so cool. I hope the Patreon grows enough that we can get grills one day.
 

Respire “Cicatrice”

Sam: Glad to see the current screamo revival finally meet up with early aughts indie rock collective nostalgia. Never got into Broken Social scene but if they sounded more like early Converge I might have had a very different experience navigating this city in my 20s. Torontopia was a scourge.

Josiah: Yo I’m pretty sure Respire is in the nation. What’s up Respire. Thank you for offering me guestlist when you last played in Montreal… I think I stayed home and did pod stuff that night. Because I figured there’d be plenty of time to catch live bands, what with the spring of 2020 just around the corner. Anyway this is the best Respire song I’ve heard yet, and the video is also so sick. There are so many people and they have so much energy and the song just sounds like the best bits of every hardcore subgenre from 1995 to 2005. LFG.
 

David Bazan “Your Bearings”

Josiah: David Bazan’s big swing with Pedro the Lion’s 2019 comeback record Phoenix certainly had its moments (and “Quietest Friend” is one of the best songs the band ever did), but it also had some problems. More specifically, it’s not Pedro the Lion if other guys are playing drums and guitar on the record. The music just wasn’t quite deliberate enough, sounding a little too restrained. In fact, David’s solo career had a similar problem up until now as he kept getting better at Protools and smoothing out his sound. But “Your Bearings” is an absolutely return to form, offering some devastating “Breadwinner You”-esque hushed tones that emphasize the weightiness of the lyrics (which were written in collaboration with Gia Margaret, another first for Bazan). The B-side of this is a Gillian Welch cover, and the whole thing seems to mark an exciting return to hushed depression for Bazan.

Sam: It’s always wild to me when I finally open my heart to something I've heard Josiah carry on about endlessly and just kind of dismissed because it's a "Josiah thing." But just like the new MXPX song I can't stop listening to, this is... extremely my shit and I intend on never stopping listening to it also.
 

Turnstile “Mystery”

Josiah: I can’t believe how long it’s been since Turnstile were here, but I’m so ready for them to come back, and this song shows why. It’s not quite Mark Palm/Ben Cook power-pop, but instead they’ve fully submersed themselves in alternative rock with a hefty alt fist pumper that’s more muscular than any of the contemporaries in the pipeline. There’s also a Corgan-esque solo, some Third Eye Blind/RHCP vocals and, most importantly, the adorable pink/Cooper font aesthetic suggests some promising merch options. Also, the synth sound at the start sees them inching ever closer to Vulfpeck territory. Thank you, Turnstile. I needed this.

Sam: I’ve always appreciated Turnstile, but they felt too cool for me to really get into. This doesn’t feel cool at all. I am finally, officially into Turnstile. It rules that all the stuff I never stopped loving is back in style thanks to young people. Does this mean I’m cool now? It’s my time.
